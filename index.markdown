Hello! I am a 4th year PhD Candidate in the Sociology Department at Cornell University, advised by [Dan Hirschman](http://danhirschman.com/).

I work in the areas of mental health, culture, and religion/spirituality and use qualitative methods, especially interviews and ethnography, to answer questions and issues at the intersection of large-scale crisis and care. A preprint of my most recent paper, “Psychotherapy in Unsettled Times: How Therapists Mediate Between Collective Crises and Personal Distress,” can be found [here](https://osf.io/preprints/socarxiv/vm7y6_v1). My research has been supported by the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship Program ([NSF GRFP](https://www.nsfgrfp.org/)), the Health Policy Research Scholars Program ([HPRS](https://healthpolicyresearch-scholars.org/scholars/christine-shio-lim/)) through the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, and the Mellon Mays Undergraduate Fellowship (MMUF).
